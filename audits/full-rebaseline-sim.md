## 4. Solo — Key Findings

### 4.1 Monster STR Double-Count Fix Impact
No pre-fix baseline data is available for direct comparison. However, the fixes applied today (monster STR double-count removal, +2 AC from starter armor fix, weapon dice corrections) collectively produced reasonable early-game win rates. The L1-5 data suggests monsters are not trivially easy, indicating the STR fix brought monsters into a sensible range rather than making them pushovers.

### 4.2 Caster Viability at L1-5
Casters remain the weakest solo classes at L1-5, as expected:
- **Mage:** 31.3% average — functional but fragile. Drops to single digits against Goblin (7%) and Worg (11%).
- **Bard:** 26.5% average — struggles badly at L1 (4% vs Goblin) but climbs to 33-76% by L5.
- **Psion:** 25.0% average — worst L1-3 performer (4-15%), but reaches 44-74% by L5.

Cantrips and L1 defensive abilities are helping somewhat (casters aren't at 0%), but they still get crushed by anything with decent AC and damage in early levels. The L5 jump (especially vs Skeleton Warrior) suggests tier 0 ability choices at L3 and L5 are having a meaningful impact.

### 4.3 Class Hierarchy
**Expected:** Warrior/Ranger > Rogue/Cleric > Mage/Bard/Psion (solo)

**Actual:**
- Warrior (59.8%) and Ranger (53.9%) are top tier. Confirmed.
- Cleric (48.5%) outperforms Rogue (39.3%). Cleric is punching above its weight — strong healing/sustain makes it a surprisingly effective solo class through L10.
- Psion (25.0%) > Bard (21.4%) > Mage (17.7%). Mage is the weakest overall. All three casters are in the expected bottom tier.

The hierarchy is broadly correct but Cleric is closer to the martial tier than the caster tier.

### 4.4 Caster Non-Viability Threshold
- **Mage:** Drops below 10% at L10 (3% vs Crypt Warden, 2% vs Basilisk). Effectively non-viable solo from L10 onward.
- **Bard:** Drops below 10% at L15 (0% vs both Hydra and Mire Hulk). Brief resurgence at L20 (53% vs Mind Flayer) then crashes again.
- **Psion:** More resilient than other casters — 56% vs Crypt Warden at L10, 63% vs Sandstorm Djinn at L20. Drops below 10% consistently from L25 onward.

### 4.5 Death Valleys
- **L15 is a catastrophic death valley.** Only Warrior can beat ANY L15 monster, and only the Mire Hulk (63%). Hydra is a near-total wipeout (only Warrior at 24%). Every other class hits 0-3% across both monsters. This is the sharpest difficulty cliff in the entire dataset.
- **L25-30 is a second death valley.** Purple Worm (0.9% avg), Dread Colossus (0%), Storm Giant (0%). Only Warrior has any traction at L25 vs Ashlands Wyrm (56%).
- **L40 Ranger anomaly.** Ranger jumps to 62-80% at L40 while every other class (except Warrior at 3-7%) is at 0%. This is a massive outlier that needs investigation — likely a spec ability or stat scaling issue unique to Ranger/Sharpshooter.

### 4.6 Monster Balance Issues
- **Skeleton Warrior (L5)** is too easy at 88% avg — most classes steamroll it. Consider as an intentional "tutorial" monster or bump its stats.
- **Hydra and Mire Hulk (L15)** are vastly overtuned for solo play. Even Warrior barely scratches Hydra. These may be intended as group-only content, but the gap between L10 (52-74% avg) and L15 (3-9% avg) is extreme.
- **L30 monsters (Dread Colossus, Storm Giant)** are completely unbeatable solo. 0% across all 7 classes, 100 iterations each. These are definitionally group-only content.
- **L40 monsters** are only beatable by Ranger. The Ranger anomaly here is suspicious.

## 6. Group — Key Findings

### 6.1 Party Size Impact
Larger parties do NOT consistently outperform smaller ones — the Duo (Warrior + Cleric) is the most resilient composition across all levels:
- **Duo** maintains 84-100% easy, 86-94% medium, and 40-84% hard across ALL levels including L30-40.
- **Full Party (Balanced)** starts strong but degrades faster: hard difficulty drops from 90% at L5 to 2% at L40.
- **Trio** performs similarly to Quartet in most brackets.

This suggests the CR matching system scales encounter difficulty with party size, but the Warrior + Cleric synergy (tank + heal) is disproportionately effective. Larger parties bring more damage but also face proportionally harder encounters.

### 6.2 Difficulty Scaling vs Targets

**Targets:** Easy ~85%, Medium ~55%, Hard ~35%

**Actual averages across all compositions and levels:**
- **Easy:** 95.5% (target 85%) — too easy by ~10 points
- **Medium:** 77.8% (target 55%) — too easy by ~23 points
- **Hard:** 44.9% (target 35%) — close to target, slightly high

Easy and medium encounters are significantly easier than design targets. The CR matching formula may be undervaluing player power, or "easy" and "medium" difficulty multipliers need to be tightened.

### 6.3 Caster Heavy vs Balanced
The caster-heavy party underperforms balanced at every level, as expected:
- L5: Nearly identical (100/94/88 vs 98/94/90)
- L10: Slightly weaker (100/88/66 vs 100/96/82)
- L15: Noticeable gap (94/58/16 vs 100/74/44)
- L20: Significant gap (86/44/0 vs 96/74/12)
- L30: Large gap (98/62/6 vs 100/84/24)
- L40: Large gap (82/42/6 vs 92/50/2)

The caster-heavy party hits 0% on hard difficulty at L20 — the earliest of any composition. The gap widens as levels increase, confirming casters scale worse than martials in group content too.

### 6.4 Group Content Becomes Mandatory for Casters
Based on solo data, casters become non-viable solo around L10-15. In groups:
- Caster-heavy groups remain viable through L15 on medium (58%) but collapse on hard (16%).
- By L20, caster-heavy groups need easy encounters to stay above 80%.
- Balanced groups carry casters effectively through L20 on medium (74%) but struggle on hard (12%).

**Recommendation:** Group content should be strongly encouraged for all players from L15 onward. Solo content at L15+ should be reserved for Warrior/Ranger with appropriate warnings.

### 6.5 CR Matching Quality
- **L5-L10:** CR matching works well. Difficulty tiers produce clear separation.
- **L15-L20:** The gap between medium and hard widens dramatically. Hard encounters at L15-20 may be overtuned for groups.
- **L30-L40:** Results are erratic. The Duo outperforms larger parties at high levels, suggesting CR scaling doesn't properly account for the tank+heal synergy. The Quartet shows unusual strength at L30 (88% medium, 54% hard) compared to adjacent compositions.
- **L30 Duo anomaly:** Medium (94%) is HIGHER than easy (84%). This indicates a possible CR matching inconsistency at L30 for 2-player parties.

### Scorecard Summary

**3 of 9 targets pass.** All failures are in the same direction: players are winning too often. The audit fixes (starter armor +2 AC, monster STR double-count removal, weapon dice corrections) collectively shifted the balance toward players.

**Root cause hypothesis:** The monster STR double-count removal made monsters significantly weaker, and the +2 AC from the starter armor fix stacked on top of this. Players are now tankier AND monsters deal less damage — a double buff. The CR formula was presumably tuned for the pre-fix (harder) monster stats and needs recalibration.

**Recommended next steps:**
1. Investigate the L40 Ranger anomaly — likely a broken Sharpshooter spec ability.
2. Investigate the L10 Mage cliff — 55% to 2% in two levels is too sharp.
3. Recalibrate CR formula difficulty multipliers: tighten "easy" (reduce from ~95% to ~85%) and "medium" (reduce from ~78% to ~55%).
4. Review L15 monsters (Hydra, Mire Hulk) — are they intentionally group-only? If so, L15 solo content needs a viable "standard" monster.
5. Review L30 monsters — total 0% solo suggests these should never appear as solo road encounters.
6. Consider a mild monster stat buff or player defense reduction to bring solo win rates back toward design targets.

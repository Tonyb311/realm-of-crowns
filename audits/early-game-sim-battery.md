- **Troll (L9 elite): 0.1% avg win** — effectively unbeatable solo. CR delta +7.6 explains it. Needs stat reduction or should only appear in group encounters.

## 3. Solo Key Findings

### Hardest levels for new players
- **L1-3 is brutal.** Average win rate across all classes is only 28-42%. New players will lose more than they win at the very start of the game.
- **L1 vs Wild Boar** is the single hardest L1 encounter: only Warriors survive consistently (72%).
- The **L3 Worg** (16% avg) is a death sentence for non-martial classes.

### Caster solo viability
- **Casters are NOT viable solo at L1-3.** Mage averages 8% against easy L1-3 monsters.
- **Casters become viable solo at L4-5** when the stat scaling starts to outpace monster HP.
- **Mage/Bard/Psion hit 0% at L10** vs both Sandscale Basilisk and Broodmother Spider — casters collapse at the T1/T2 boundary.
- The **Slime (L2) is an anomaly**: Cleric beats it 69% but Rogue gets 0%. Slime may have mechanics that counter physical classes.

### New vs existing monsters
- New L1 monsters (Wild Boar, Goblin) produce comparable win rates to existing ones.
- **Wild Boar is harder than Goblin** at L1 (28% vs 42%) — appropriate since it has higher HP (18 vs 24).
- **Alpha Wolf (L4 elite, new) at 63%** is actually easier than Sand Viper (L4 standard) at 65%. The "elite" tag doesn't make it harder — both are within normal range.

### Elite difficulty
- **Alpha Wolf (L4 elite): 63%** — appropriately tough, good fight.
- **Ghoul Pack Leader (L5 elite): 46%** — challenging, good design.
- **Bandit Captain (L6 elite): 90%** — too easy for an elite after our nerf. Consider reverting some stats.
- **Hooktusk (L7 elite): 11%** — too hard solo, fine for groups.
- **Troll (L9 elite): 0%** — broken solo, must be group-only.

### Death valley
- **L1-3 is a death valley for all casters** (Mage, Bard, Psion average <15% win rate).
- **L6 Orc Warrior** creates a spike (21% avg) — martial classes drop to 50% and casters to 0%.
- **L10 is a caster wall** — Mage/Bard/Psion hit 0% against both L10 monsters.

## 5. Group Key Findings

### Party size impact
- **Duo is surprisingly viable.** War+Cleric handles easy (94-100%) and medium (82-92%) comfortably at all levels.
- **Trio struggles with hard difficulty** — drops to 38-54% at L5+. The lack of a 4th member matters.
- **Quartet is the sweet spot** — 96-100% easy, 96-98% medium, 64-72% hard. This is the ideal party size.
- **Full parties (5 members)** can handle hard difficulty (76-92% balanced, 52-78% caster heavy).

### Duo viability
- **Easy encounters: 94-100%** — Duos can farm easy content reliably.
- **Medium encounters: 82-92%** — Duos are viable but take some losses.
- **Hard encounters: 54-84%** — Variable. L5 hard is the toughest at 54%.

### Hard difficulty viability
- **Duo hard: 54-84%** — viable but risky. Good reward/risk ratio.
- **Trio hard: 38-54%** — marginal. Below 40% at L8 (the only balance alert).
- **Quartet hard: 64-72%** — comfortable.
- **Full hard: 76-92% (balanced) / 52-78% (caster)** — dominant.

### Caster Heavy vs Balanced
- **Caster Heavy is 10-20% worse than Balanced** across the board.
- L8 hard: Caster Heavy 52% vs Balanced 78% — significant gap.
- More deaths per fight (3.1 avg vs 2.5 avg at hard difficulty).
- Still viable (>50% at hard) but clearly weaker. The Ranger anchor helps somewhat.

### CR matching quality
- **Easy: 94-100%** — target ~80-90%, slightly too easy
- **Medium: 72-98%** — target ~50-60%, significantly too easy
- **Hard: 38-92%** — target ~30-40%, about right for smaller parties, too easy for full parties

## 6. Retention Risk Assessment

### L1-3 Solo (Retention Critical)
- **Warrior: 74% easy, 49% hard** — good starting experience.
- **Ranger: 63% easy, 41% hard** — acceptable.
- **Rogue: 24% easy** — DANGER. New Rogue players will feel powerless.
- **Cleric: 42% easy** — borderline. Wins enough to not quit but frustrating.
- **Mage: 8% easy** — CRITICAL. L1-3 Mage loses 92% of easy encounters. Players will quit.
- **Bard: 14% easy** — CRITICAL. Same problem.
- **Psion: 13% easy** — CRITICAL. Same problem.

**Retention risk: HIGH for caster classes (Mage/Bard/Psion).** Players who roll a caster will lose nearly every fight until L4. Either casters need early-game buffs, L1-3 monsters need stat reductions, or the game needs to explicitly warn that casters are group-dependent.

### L1-3 Group
- **Duo easy: 94%** — excellent. Players can group up immediately and succeed.
- **Trio easy: 100%** — even better.
- Groups solve the caster viability problem at L1-3.

### L4-6 Difficulty Spike
- **L4 is a massive power spike** — players jump from 28-42% to 63-65%. This feels like reward for surviving early levels.
- **L6 Orc Warrior (21% avg)** is a local difficulty spike. Not a retention risk since L6 Bandit Captain (90%) is easy — players just need to avoid Orc territory.

### L7-10 T2 Preparation
- **L7 Hooktusk (11%)** and **L9 Troll (0%)** are effective gatekeepers.
- Martial classes handle L7-9 content well (57-100% against standard monsters).
- **L10 is a caster cliff** — Mage/Bard hit 0% against both L10 monsters. Casters absolutely need groups for T2.
